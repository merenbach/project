The affine cipher, a monoalphabetic substitution cipher, makes use of an intriguing multiplier and additive to generate letters based upon a simple formula (which varies based upon the multiplier and additive). This also represents its downfall, for if an attacker finds two letters, the whole formula can be discerned.
